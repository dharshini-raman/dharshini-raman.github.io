Reflections from My Senior Year

Tech internships, problem-solving, and the creative side of computer science.

Posted by Dharshini Raman on September 26, 2024

This year has been a whirlwind of challenges and learning experiences. From interning at Apple to Capital One, I’ve worked on impactful projects that gave me a deeper appreciation for both software development and creative problem-solving in technology.

Being a Computer Science student at UNC has allowed me to explore various areas of tech, but it was my internship experiences that really put my skills to the test. Whether it was optimizing code for performance or contributing to team-wide projects, each step was an opportunity to grow.

One of my favorite aspects of software development is the ability to collaborate with others to turn an idea into a functional solution. Working in different tech environments has made me realize how creativity and problem-solving truly go hand in hand.

I’ve always believed that computer science is a creative outlet. It’s not just about writing code; it’s about designing innovative solutions that can change the way we interact with the world.

What's Next?

As graduation approaches, I’m excited to see where my journey in tech takes me next. With the foundation I’ve built through internships and coursework, I’m eager to continue growing as a software engineer and problem-solver. The possibilities are endless, and I’m ready to dive into them headfirst.

"The more I learn, the more I realize how much I don't know. And that’s what makes this journey so exciting!"

As I move forward, I’m looking forward to exploring new technologies, working with talented people, and making an impact in the world of tech.

Problem-solving is not just a technical skill; it's an art.

Stay tuned for more reflections on life, tech, and the endless pursuit of knowledge as I wrap up my time at UNC and take on new challenges in the tech world.
